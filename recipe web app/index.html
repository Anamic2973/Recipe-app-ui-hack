Classic Beef Stew with Garden Vegetables

A hearty and flavorful beef stew with aromatic herbs and seasonal vegetables, perfect for cool evenings.

Prep Time

20 minutes

Cook Time

2 hours

Servings

6

Difficulty

Medium

Ingredients

US Metric
2 pounds beef chuck, cut into 1-inch cubes
1/4 cup all-purpose flour
2 tablespoons olive oil
2 tablespoons butter
1 large onion, chopped
3 cloves garlic, minced
1 cup red wine (optional)
4 cups beef broth
2 tablespoons tomato paste
1 bay leaf
1 teaspoon dried thyme
3 medium carrots, sliced
2 celery stalks, sliced
1 pound baby potatoes, halved
1 cup frozen peas
Salt and pepper to taste
Freshly chopped parsley for garnish

Instructions

1 Season beef chunks with salt and pepper, then toss with flour until lightly coated.
2 In a large Dutch oven or heavy pot, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Add beef in batches (don't overcrowd) and brown on all sides, about 5 minutes per batch. Transfer to a plate.
3 In the same pot, add butter. Once melted, add onions and cook until softened, about 3 minutes. Add garlic and cook for another minute.
4 Pour in wine (if using) and scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. Let simmer for 2 minutes.
5 Return beef to the pot. Add beef broth, tomato paste, bay leaf, and thyme. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 1 hour.
6 Add carrots, celery, and potatoes. Continue to simmer, covered, until vegetables and beef are tender, about 45-60 minutes more.
7 Stir in frozen peas during the last 5 minutes of cooking.
8 Remove bay leaf. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper if needed.
9 Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley.
